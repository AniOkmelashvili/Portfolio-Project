## Summary  
Simmonds explains that AI visibility is not a standalone channel but the result of accumulated distribution efforts such as SEO, community participation, reviews, and third-party content. He introduces the idea that modern buyer journeys are split across AI discovery, review platforms, and owned content.

## Key Insight  
- AI systems aggregate signals from across the entire digital footprint  
- Visibility is built through long-term distribution, not single-channel efforts  
- Trust signals (reviews, mentions, comparisons) are critical for visibility  
- Buyer journeys are now multi-layered (AI → evaluation → retention)  
- Brand authority depends on consistency across owned and third-party platforms  

## Deeper Interpretation  
This post reframes AI visibility as an outcome of ecosystem-wide marketing rather than a direct optimization channel. Instead of focusing on isolated tactics like SEO or content marketing, Simmonds emphasizes integrated distribution strategy. The core idea is that modern brand authority is built through accumulated trust signals across multiple platforms that influence both humans and AI systems.

## Takeaway  
AI-era marketing success depends on building a consistent, multi-channel trust footprint rather than optimizing a single distribution channel.
