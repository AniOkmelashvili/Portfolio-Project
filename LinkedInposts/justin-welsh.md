## Summary  
Welsh argues that true business success is not about maximizing revenue, but about designing a sustainable and flexible lifestyle. He emphasizes autonomy, reduced operational stress, and personal fulfillment over aggressive scaling.

## Key Insight  
- Business success can be defined beyond revenue growth  
- Autonomy and flexibility are key indicators of success for many founders  
- Scaling does not always correlate with improved quality of life  
- Sustainable business models prioritize time, freedom, and wellbeing  
- External definitions of success often conflict with personal fulfillment  

## Deeper Interpretation  
This post reframes entrepreneurship from a growth-maximization model to a lifestyle optimization model. Instead of focusing on scaling metrics, Welsh emphasizes personal alignment and operational simplicity. The underlying idea is that success is subjective and should be defined by individual life design rather than external benchmarks.

## Takeaway  
True business success is defined by alignment between work, freedom, and personal life rather than revenue scale alone.
