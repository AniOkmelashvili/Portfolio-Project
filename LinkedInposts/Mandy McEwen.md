## Summary  
McEwen shares her early-stage experience of acquiring her first clients through cold email by offering performance-based SEO services. She explains how this strategy helped her build an agency and highlights that cold outreach still works when executed correctly and combined with social selling.

## Key Insight  
- Cold email can still be an effective client acquisition channel when done strategically  
- Early-stage growth often relies on creative offer structuring (risk-reversal, performance-based deals)  
- Combining cold outreach with social selling increases response rates  
- Iterative learning and persistence are key in early business development  
- Simple, direct value propositions outperform complex messaging in outreach  

## Deeper Interpretation  
This post demonstrates the fundamentals of early-stage B2B acquisition strategy: identifying a target niche, creating a compelling low-risk offer, and leveraging direct outreach to generate initial traction. The core principle is that execution quality matters more than channel saturation. It also highlights the importance of combining multiple acquisition channels (email + social) for improved conversion efficiency.

## Takeaway  
Effective cold outreach relies on clear value propositions, strategic targeting, and persistence rather than scale or automation alone.
