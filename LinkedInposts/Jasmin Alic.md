## Summary  
Alić provides structured feedback on LinkedIn’s comment system, suggesting improvements for usability, engagement, and analytics while also highlighting features he considers unnecessary or harmful.

## Key Insight  
- Creator experience on platforms is shaped heavily by comment functionality  
- Engagement tools (pins, threads, notifications) directly affect content quality and usability  
- Product improvements should focus on clarity, organization, and creator control  
- Not all engagement features are valuable; some can reduce content quality  

## Deeper Interpretation  
This post demonstrates product-thinking from a power user perspective. Instead of general complaints, Alić provides structured, categorized feedback (what to add vs what to avoid). This reflects how creators think about platform optimization — balancing engagement growth with usability and content quality. It also highlights how creator feedback can influence product evolution in B2B/social platforms.

## Takeaway  
High-quality platform engagement depends on well-designed interaction systems that support creators without introducing unnecessary noise or complexity.
