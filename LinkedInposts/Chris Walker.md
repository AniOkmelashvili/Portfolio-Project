## Summary  
Walker explains two contrasting ways people relate to money: as a measure of self-worth or as a byproduct of creating value. He argues that internal identity determines financial behavior and outcomes more than external strategy.

## Key Insight  
- Money can be perceived either as validation or as a tool  
- External validation-based identity leads to dissatisfaction  
- Value-driven mindset focuses on impact rather than comparison  
- Internal self-worth influences financial decision-making and outcomes  

## Deeper Interpretation  
This post frames financial behavior as an identity issue rather than a technical or strategic one. Walker suggests that mindset determines long-term financial success by shaping intentions, consistency, and decision-making. The underlying idea is that internal alignment leads to more sustainable and effective external results.

## Takeaway  
Financial outcomes are influenced more by internal identity and mindset than by external accumulation strategies.
